Glossary

AOD

Accounting Of Disclosures. A list of recipients to whom patient healthcare information has been given for purposes other than treatment, payment, or healthcare operations, as mandated by the Privacy Rule of the Health Insurance Portability and Accountability Act (HIPAA) legislation.

Attachments

An attachment is a document from an external clinical system that you have added to a patient's OneContent record. The document may be electronically downloaded from a clinical system that has been integrated with the ROI Module, or it may be acquired from your local drive, a network drive, or a portable drive. In any case, once the file is attached to the patient's record, a copy of it is stored in the OneContent file system.

Attachments can be associated with an existing or non-existing patient. They are listed in the document tree like any other document and can be added to ROI requests. An attachment can be deleted from the patient record or the request as long as it has not been released. Once released, attachments are permanently associated with the patient record.

The release of attachments can be audited and is included in the Accounting of Disclosures report with the number of pages and release history.

Authorization Request

The form that captures the request for records submitted by a requestor. Triggers the creation of a request within the ROI Module.

Billing tier

A defined charge structure. Billing for a request can contain one to many billing tiers ("Electronic" is always included).

Certification letter

A letter created by the facility, in response to the request for medical records, attesting to the authenticity of the records as being true and accurate.

DAR

Documents Available for Release. Refers to the document tree in the Patient(s) Selected for this Request area on the Requests > Patient Information screen.

Disclosure date

The date the patient's documents were disclosed (that is, released).

Disclosure document

A form completed by the patient to identify one or more entities from which to withhold all or part of the patient's records. The facility cannot release the patient's documents to any entity listed on the form.

Directory Services Authentication

A user authentication method that requires your Windows login credentials instead of your system-based user ID and password. Available only with ROI Module Release 15.0 or later.

DSR

Refers to the document tree in the Document(s) Selected for Release box on the Requests > Patient Information screen.

EMPI

Enterprise Master Person Index. An ID that uniquely identifies a person across all enterprise systems and facilities.

EPN

Enterprise Person Number.An identifier assigned to a patient by the enterprise and that is used across enterprise facilities. Requires the enterprise to be EMPI-enabled.

Facility

Location where patient care occurred.

Patient

A patient whose records exist in the standard OneContent database tables by virtue of an ADT electronic feed or a scanning process. You cannot add, change, or delete the patient information.

Invoice

Billing for released documents.

Invoice Type

Indicates whether an invoice is a pre-bill invoice (created from the pre-bill operation) or an invoice (created from the invoice operation).

Locked status

Patient records that are locked against access by unauthorized users. In the ROI Module, locked patients and/or encounters are hidden from users without proper access permissions, and the system displays an alert that some records were filtered out due to restrictions. For users with access to locked records, locked patients and/or encounters are displayed and indicated as locked.

Masking

In ROI, the practice of substituting asterisks (*) for legitimate characters to keep the characters suppressed (for example, used to suppress Social Security Number characters to keep the SSN private).

MRN

Patient's Medical Record Number. For sites that are not EMPI-enabled, the Facility + MRN are used as the patient identifier in patient summary information (patients demographic information on a request) instead of EPN. Within the OneContent application, a patient may have a different MRN at each facility.

Non-MPF document

A document that has no document image in the database. ROI users must still account for releasing copies of such a document, so must create and maintain a record of it.

Non-MPF patient

A patient for whom no record currently exists in the standard database tables. If you have proper security permissions, you can add, change, or delete information and/or documents for non-sytem patients. Non-system patient information is stored in standard database tables that are ROI-specific.

Non-MPF patient ID

Uniquely identifies a non-system patient.

Patient identifier

A standard patient identifier in lieu of EPN. Derived from Facility + MRN.

Patient type

The type of encounter (for example, inpatient, outpatient, or emergency room). Defined within the OneContent application.

Pre-Bill letter

A letter or invoice generated and sent to the requestor before the ROI request is fulfilled. Must be paid before the request is fulfilled.

Record view

A collection of document types. Configured in the OneContent application.

Requestor

The person or organization that submits a request for patient records.

Requestor type

A category of requestor (for example, Patient, Attorney, VA, and so on) that is configured within the ROI Module. Can be selected to associate with a requestor.

TPO

Treatment/Payment/Healthcare Operations.

VIP status

The status given to non-system patients, or to encounters for patients, who have requested that access to their records be restricted. ROI users must have the ROI VIP Status permission to access such records. For users who do not have access to VIP records, the system displays an alert at the request level that some records were filtered out due to VIP restrictions.
