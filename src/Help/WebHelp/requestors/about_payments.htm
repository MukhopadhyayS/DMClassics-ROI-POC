About payments

Authorized users can post one or more payments to a requestor's account and individual invoices.  

  • Payments can be posted both before and after documents are released (such as when a pre-bill has been generated and payment must first be made).  
  • Multiple payments can be made using a variety of payment methods: check, cash, credit card.  Each of these payments must be posted separately.  
  • Payments can be added to an account that has a zero balance or credit.
  • Payments cannot be added to an invoice that has a zero balance.
  • Payments can be removed for an open invoice.  

Payments can be applied in the following ways:

  • Manually to one or more invoices
  • Manually to the requestor's account

Payment Types

There are two types of payments: applied and unapplied.

  • Applied payments can be associated with a single invoice or distributed across multiple invoices.
  • Unapplied payments made to a requestor's account are considered "available funds." This type of payment remains in the requestor's account until it is applied.  It is included in the Liability section of reports labeled as Unapplied Payments and indicates that funds exist that may need to be applied to an account. This payment can be applied manually or automatically during the release and invoice process in the Unapplied Adjustments/Payments dialog box.

Another consideration is a prepayment against an ROI request. When a prepayment is received, the system posts the received amount in cash or credit (depending upon the payment method), and creates a liability that represents the prepayment. When the records are released, the system shows the prepayment as income for the entire amount and any discounts, plus it removes the liability for the prepayment. If additional money is received at the time the records are released, then the additional payment is posted in cash or credit. Any remaining amount that is owed shows in accounts receivable.
