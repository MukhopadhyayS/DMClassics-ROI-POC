About patient records

The Patients tab enables you to view information and request history for both MPF and non-MPF patients.  

  • An "MPF patient" is one who already exists in the standard MPF database tables by virtue of an ADT electronic feed or a scanning process. You cannot add, change, or delete MPF patient information.

  • A "non-MPF patient" is one for whom no record currently exists in the standard MPF database tables. If you have proper security permissions, you can add, change, or delete information and/or documents for non-MPF patients. Non-MPF patient information is stored in MPF database tables that are specific to ROI.

  • A "non-MPF document" is one that has no document image in the MPF database. ROI users must still account for releasing copies of such a document, so must create and maintain a record of it. A non-MPF patient can have zero to many non-MPF documents, but a non-MPF document is associated with a single patient.

Patient records

Patient records include:

  • Identification data, such as name, address, date of birth, MRN, and so on, that is stored in the McKesson Patient Folder database.

  • Documents about the patient, including Meaningful Use documents and attachments.

    Meaningful Use documents refers to Clinical Summaries (CCRs/CCDs), Discharge Summaries, and Discharge Instructions. Attachments refers to Clinical Summaries that are added to the patient's record.

Access to patient records

Your MPF Workstation user profile controls your ability to access documents and ROI functions.

If you do not have certain permissions, some buttons and menu options are hidden. If you cannot perform an operation, you most likely do not have appropriate permissions, and you should consult your system administrator. If you are logged into ROI when rights are granted, you must log out and log back in for the change to take effect.

To minimize the chance of requests being fulfilled with missing documents, McKesson recommends that all ROI users have access to all facilities in the enterprise and have a record view group with access to all document types.

Searching for records

You can use search criteria in any combination, but these fields have specific requirements:

  • You must enter at least three characters in Last Name or First Name or across both fields.

  • Date of Birth must be combined with at least one other field.

  • Facility must be combined with at least one other field.

  • When used, the EPN, SSN, MRN, and Enc/Acct fields each require a minimum of three characters.

Note: Non-EPN systems can list the same patient multiple times if the patient has encounters at different facilities. The Facility and MRN numbers combine to make a unique patient ID, even though the same patient is involved. An EPN system, on the other hand, will have only one entry for a patient who has encounters at different facilities.

Requests for records

Patients as well as outside agencies can request copies of records.

Disclosure documents enable patients to identify one or more entities from which to withhold all or part of the patient record. Generally, ROI personnel adhere to instructions provided by the patient on the disclosure form to withhold records.

Auditing

Auditing is controlled by MPF Workstation settings for each facility.

If the corresponding codes are enabled for the facility in MPF Workstation, the system audits these events:

  • Changing information for an existing  patient (code 2 must be enabled)

  • Creating a new non-MPF patient (code 2 must be enabled)

  • Searching for patients (code B must be enabled)

  • Printing and viewing patient documents (codes P and V must be enabled)
